The Background of the Movie

The Steve Jobs movie that starred Ashton Kutcher was released in 2013. It was directed by Joshua Michael Stern and written by Matt Whiteley. The film was an independent production that aimed to depict the life of Steve Jobs, the co-founder of Apple Inc.

Kutcher's Performance

Ashton Kutcher played the role of Steve Jobs in the movie. He portrayed the character of Jobs quite convincingly. He had the physical resemblance to Jobs, and his acting was impressive. Kutcher portrayed the complex emotions and the genius of Jobs with ease.

The Plot

The movie tells the story of Steve Jobs' life from his college days until the launch of the iPod in 2001. The plot focuses on Jobs' personality, his relationship with his colleagues, and his struggles with his personal life. The film also shows the development of Apple Inc. from a startup to a tech giant.

The Good Points of the Movie

Accurate Depiction of Steve Jobs

The movie does an excellent job of portraying Steve Jobs' personality and his genius. The film shows Jobs' perfectionism, his vision, and his obsession with design. It also depicts his difficult personality and his sometimes strained relationship with his colleagues.

Great Cinematography

The movie's cinematography is fantastic. The film features beautiful shots of California in the 70s and 80s, which gives the audience a glimpse of the time period. The scenes shot inside Apple's offices and at product launches are also impressive.

The Soundtrack

The movie's soundtrack is excellent. It features music from the time period, including songs from Bob Dylan and The Beatles. The music adds to the movie's authenticity and helps to transport the audience back to the 70s and 80s.

The Bad Points of the Movie

The Accuracy of the Storyline

While the movie does an excellent job of portraying Steve Jobs, some aspects of the storyline are not entirely accurate. The film takes some creative liberties, which may not sit well with Jobs' fans.

The Pacing of the Movie

The movie's pacing is slow at times. Some scenes feel like they drag on for too long, which may bore the audience.

The Supporting Cast

While Ashton Kutcher's performance was impressive, some of the supporting cast's acting fell short. Some of the performances felt wooden and lacked depth.

The Verdict

Overall, the Steve Jobs movie starring Ashton Kutcher is a good film. It accurately portrays Steve Jobs' personality and genius, and the cinematography and soundtrack are excellent. While there are some inaccuracies in the storyline, the movie is an enjoyable watch. However, the pacing can be slow at times, and some of the supporting cast's performances lack depth.
